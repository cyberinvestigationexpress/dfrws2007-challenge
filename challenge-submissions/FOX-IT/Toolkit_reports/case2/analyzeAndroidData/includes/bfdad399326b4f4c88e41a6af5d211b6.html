Experience Thursdays in the Square
 
Spring is in full bloom, which means it's that time of year again: Market Square welcomes family-friendly activities including one of Pittsburgh's favorite farmers markets.
 
From 10 a.m. - 2 p.m. each Thursday, May 12 - November 17, enjoy over 50 varieties of produce, beautiful flower bouquets, fresh pies and more at the Market Square Farmers Market. Meet friends for lunch at one of the outdoor cafes or purchase hot foods from the vendors and sit at one of the colorful umbrella tables in the center of the Square—it's a fun and lively atmosphere you won't want to miss! 
 
With a dozen vendors offering goods of all kinds, there  is something at the Market for everyone. And Thursdays are not the only event days in Market Square. The Tuesday Reading Room begins on May 17 and Friday KidsPlay begins on June 3. We'll see you in the Square.
Run the City of Bridges:
The Dick's Sporting Goods Pittsburgh Marathon 
  
The 2011 Marathon, slated for May 15, begins  Downtown and winds through Pittsburgh neighborhoods, taking runners on a 26.2 mile journey of the beautiful Steel City.
 
 
Beginning at 6 a.m. and with seven hours to complete the Marathon, participants will be greeted by volunteers, bands, and other treats along the route. With staying on course firmly in mind, the extracurriculars may be more for onlookers than the participants.
 
 
 
While the Marathon may be sold out to walkers and runners, volunteers are still needed. You can join the Pittsburgh Downtown Partnership's volunteer team and cheer runners to the finish line. Be a part of a winning team - the Dick's Sporting Goods
Pittsburgh Marathon team. 
10th Annual Great Outdoors Week 
Friday, May 13 - Sunday, May 22, 2011

Whether you're new to the outdoors scene or a seasoned veteran, you'll have plenty of activities to choose from during this ten day celebration!

Five major events, the Dick’s Sporting Goods Pittsburgh Marathon, National Bike to Work Day, Learn to Row and Paddle, Venture Outdoors Festival and Pedal Pittsburgh are among the more than 60 events planned by local organizations throughout Southwestern Pennsylvania.
 


Great Outdoors Week showcases our region as a hub for outdoor recreation activities - biking, hiking, paddling and much more. Grab your friends or coworkers, pack up the family - get ready for some fun that's close to home!
 
One of the plusses of GOW is that children and adults of any skill level, from novices to pros, can get a lot out of the activities planned. Many of the activities are free or low-cost, and some do not require any pre-registration.

“Many Great Outdoors Week events serve as a safe and easy introduction to kid-friendly activities here,” said Ginette Walker Vinski of Sustainable Pittsburgh, the group that coordinates Great Outdoors Week with the help of a network of outdoor recreation partners.

“Allowing kids time to play in nature is greatly important to their health and development. Utilization of our natural resources, like our trails, waterways and parks, helps kids develop a sense of respect and value for these places.”

 In 2008, Sustainable Pittsburgh and its partners launched Walls are Bad, a campaign designed to increase awareness and participation in the many outdoor recreation activities available in
Southwestern Pennsylvania.

Cirque Du Soleil Presents TOTEM

TOTEM traces the fascinating journey of the human species from its original amphibian state to its ultimate desire to fly. The characters evolve on a stage evoking a giant turtle, the symbol of origin for many ancient civilizations.

Inspired by many founding myths, TOTEM illustrates, through a visual and acrobatic language, the evolutionary progress of species.
 
 
Somewhere between science and legend TOTEM explores the ties that bind Man to other species, his dreams and his infinite potential.

Silk Screen Asian American Film Festival 
 
Silk Screen
's mission is to celebrate diversity and multi-cultural appreciation of the Asian and Asian American experience through cultural events.
 
 
Silk Screen's goals include educating communities about Asian and Asian American history, culture, experiences and issues, fostering understanding across lines of race, ethnicity, religion, age, and region, and sustaining growth and encouraging excellence in Asian American culture.
 
Almost two dozen films comprise this year's festival. See Silk Screen's website for film times and locations.
